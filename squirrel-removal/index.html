Identifying Entry Points

Squirrels can squeeze through surprisingly small openings. We look for chewed holes around rooflines, eaves, vents, chimneys, and any gaps or cracks in the foundation or siding. Understanding how they're getting in is paramount to stopping future access.

Squirrels often build nests, or 'dreys,' in attics, wall cavities, or even sheds and garages. Finding the nest helps us understand the scope of the infestation and whether there are any young present, which requires a different removal strategy.

Squirrels love to chew. They can damage electrical wiring, insulation, wooden structures, and even plumbing. Identifying the extent of the damage is important for recommending necessary repairs after removal.

Based on the inspection findings, we develop a tailored removal plan. This plan considers the number of squirrels, their location, the presence of young, and the specific characteristics of your property. We utilize a variety of humane techniques.

For many situations, live trapping is the preferred method. We use species-specific traps placed strategically near entry points or nesting areas. Once captured, the squirrels are safely relocated in accordance with local regulations, far from your property.

Exclusion Techniques

Preventing future entry is key. After removal, we seal all identified entry points using durable, squirrel-proof materials. This might involve repairing damaged areas, installing mesh over vents, or reinforcing weak spots that could be exploited.

Habitat Modification

Sometimes, simply making your property less attractive to squirrels can help. This might involve trimming tree branches that provide easy access to your roof or securing garbage cans. We'll provide recommendations specific to your property.
